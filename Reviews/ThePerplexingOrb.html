The Perplexing Orb Review

Published: 4/4/2016

Release Date: 1/14/2016

Played On: Wii U

The perplexing orb is a game about a ball trying to restore energy to an extinct tribe.  It goes from one level to the next hitting totem poles and then it finds a member of the tribe the end.  There is nothing here to show the player the story aside from a text box at the beginning of the game!  Why does a game in which you roll a ball around need an arbitrarily tacked on story?  We’ll get to that later.  

The visuals are garbage. Everything about this game screams college freshman Unity project.  Textures are boring and basic, objects just float in the air and seriously, watch what happens when you hit a treasure chest!

The whole game is just lazy.  I get that visuals like this can work in a game where there’s a stylistic reason for them, but in this game, you don’t feel like this was intentional.  You feel like this was all the developers were capable of making.  It doesn’t help that the music is both repetitive and annoying.  And the sound effects are straight up stock sounds that don’t fit the object you’re collecting at all.  Even the menu is barebones and has some major problems, and I’ll mention that eventually, but first...

    The game works as expected.  Move the ball along the platforms and hit the totem poles at the end.  Collect these objects and treasure chests along the way.  It controls as expected, which is the only decent thing about the game, and even that has some issues.  There are no physics for this ball.  You float around in the air and slide around when moving.  There are levels in which you slide on ice and it’s nearly impossible to see the effect it has because the ball has a crazy amount of momentum already.  That being said, the ball goes in the direction you press when moving the control stick.

But there are other issues with the game too.  Let’s do this rapid-fire style. 

  1. If you go over a ledge like this, you can’t see the ball anymore.

  2. There’s no control over the camera which makes moving towards yourself difficult since you can’t see what’s coming!!

  3. The text telling you the number of objects collected in a level is the same color as the fucking sky!

  4. MAZE LEVELS?! REALLY? Just to force me to play this shit even longer?

  5. There’s no ability to replay specific levels.  If you want to get all the objects, you have to get all of them in a specific world and play that world over and over again.

  6. Losing all of your lives causes you to restart the level, but you have to click continue before seeing the loading screen.  Why even have lives then?!

  7. Speaking of loading screens, WHY?! I could run this game on my N64 what the fuck are you loading?!

  8. There is nothing “Perplexing” about this orb!

  9. I spent money on this when I could have donated it to a school or something.

  10. Is that a background or a crayon drawing?

  11. Is that their company logo in the background?!

  12. I took a rocket! ...And I hit my head on the next platform and can’t find the totem pole because it shot me too high.

  13. My friend and I played the multiplayer and finished all 4 levels in 5 minutes.

  14. This is a wasted opportunity to use the Wii U gamepad for either a top-down view or possibly as a control scheme to roll the ball.  That’s not in here because that would be unique and original.

  15. Heyy, world 5 might be kind of fun, like I’d love to maybe play this like a 3D doodle jump and OH MY GOD IT’S OVER IN 5 LEVELS?!


    And of course, many more.  And aside from all of the other flaws this game presents the player, it also commits the cardinal sin of allowing you delete your saved content by starting a new game without warning you.  The game doesn’t even ask if that’s what you want to do.  I assumed there were multiple save files and it would ask me after finishing the tutorial which slot to save in, but in the end I lost all my progress because I went through the tutorial again to get footage.  The reason I had to do this is because there’s no level select, only a world select and the beginning isn’t in it.  I also finished the game in about a half hour.  A half hour?!  Are you fucking kidding me?  Given all of the flaws I’ve mentioned so far, you might think I’d be glad because that means there’s less of this game to play, and you’d be right.  That being said, given how simplistic everything is, the developers surely could have shit out at least a few more levels, but no, no of course not.  Pathetic.

The cherry on top of this shit sundae is that at the end of the game, you find this monstrosity.  This is a member of the “lost tribe” you were clearly looking for this whole time and he’s also… The main character of Treefall studio’s next game, the Last Tiki.  That’s right.  This whole game’s purpose, and the entire reason they tacked on a bullshit story at the beginning, is just to advertise their next game!  That’s so sad and delusional that it’s difficult to fathom. DO NOT PLAY THIS GAME.  DO NOT GIVE TREEFALL STUDIOS YOUR MONEY.  And apparently, nobody has since their Kickstarter for The Last Tiki failed magnificently.  You can find a better version of this game online for free! The sad thing is, I can see from their company’s YouTube videos that they do enjoy making games, but they have no dedication to what they produce.  No soul, no fun.  Looking at their Kickstarter page and their release schedule really shows a lot.  This company puts out so many games each year, but doesn’t take the time to make them worth playing.  And unfortunately, although I can see the potential that Treefall has, the lack of effort, originality, and self-awareness that comes with releasing a game like this makes any effort they did put towards the game seem like a cash grab.  Which is why I’m giving the Perplexing piece of shit a 1 out of 10.  I hope none of you play this game, I really do, because I want you all to have a mighty nifty day today!

The Perplexing Orb

The Perplexing Orb is a tech demo. A high school scenior's first project in Unity. The idea that anyone would charge money for this game isn't just ridiculous, it's insult to those that buy it and others who put actual time, work, and dedication in the games they create.
